Phase 1: The Somatic Lock (Days 1–7)

Objective: Master the exact physical-to-mental threshold pressure of the finger anchor.

  • The Ghost Movement Calibration: Alternate moving your index and middle fingers, but reduce the movement until your fingers do not physically move at all.
  • The WBTB Back-Cut: Set your alarm for exactly 4.5 hours after sleep onset. Disable it immediately upon waking.
  • The 30-Second Drill: Immediately begin the ghost movements. Count cycles up to 20, then perform the nose-pinch reality check.

Phase 2: Threshold Management (Days 8–14)

Objective: Navigate the "Vibe Shift"—the exact moment your brain transitions from physical to dream input.

  • Sensory Deprivation Priming: Lay flat on your back for 2 minutes without moving before starting your finger anchors.
  • Mapping the Shift: Look for transitional markers like auditory static, rushing wind, or physical vibrations.
  • Passive Observation: Do not panic or react to hypnagogia. Treat it like a background movie to prevent cortisol spikes.

Phase 3: Visual Phasing (Days 15–21)

Objective: Graduate from physical movements to visual, mental phasing.

  • The Synesthesia Shift: Start ghost movements for 15s. Stop physically, then visualize the movement perfectly behind your eyelids.
  • Environment Siphoning: Once the phantom movement is autonomous, force your mind to expect a specific scene to form around your moving hands.

Phase 4: High-Fidelity Architecting (Days 22–30)

Objective: Unbroken conscious awareness from waking into a pre-designed environment.

  • Pre-Rendering the Matrix: Sketch or deeply visualize a detailed "spawn point" before bed.
  • The Vector Projection: Skip finger movements. Project consciousness directly into your spawn point during WBTB.
  • The Continuous Thread: Maintain internal counting to prevent slipping into standard narrative dreaming.
